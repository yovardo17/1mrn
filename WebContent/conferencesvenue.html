Paipa, Boyacá, Colombia

It is a municipality made by the Gods with a perfect nature for people, where the rhythm becomes vibrant, fast, full of fantasy and intensity and you can experience everything you want. Everything is nearby, just beside you; you only must make the decision.

The Paipa municipality, founded in 1602, was the place of some of the most relevant events that have marked Colombian history. Colombian municipality is located in central-eastern Colombia; administratively, it is located in the province of Tundama in the department of Boyacá. Paipa, has an area of approximately 395 square kilometers, at an altitude average of 2,500 m meters about sea level, a temperature of around 14 °C depending on the month of the year, and has approximately a population of 34,000 inhabitants.

Paipa is a tourist town par excellence, famous for its thermal waters to which they attribute therapeutic benefits, the goodness of its climate, and the beauty of its landscapes; therefore, Paipa is one of the main tourist centers of Boyacá, Colombia.

In the Paipa municipality, the climate sets the changes mood to make this, municipality the friendliest, most colorful, and most charming in Colombia. Paipa is a municipality with an efficient infrastructure, that has an abundance of natural environment parks, libraries, museums, and public spaces where many cultural events and festivals take place; furthermore, Paipa is the municipality with the best hotel infrastructure in the department of Boyacá; also, due to its geographic location and natural environment, the municipality of Paipa is the perfect place to find tranquility, rest, and recreation.

What to eat?

In Paipa municipality, there is a variety of gastronomic food national and international that is very delicious; the cuisine of Paipa municipality is a cultural gastronomic legacy; strongly influenced by Spaniards and Africans, which is the result of an exquisite combination of dishes based on regional ingredients. The typical food is the "Cocido boyacense" (it is made with pork, beef, chicken, and tubers such as turnips, "chuguas" and "ibias"; likewise, it is traditional to consume meals with arepas (it is made with corn flour precooked or milled corn, among other ingredients). it is common to accompany the breakfast, with a wide variety of sweet and savory baked goods (it is including "almojábana", yucca bread, cheese bread, "buñuelos", and pastries) with coffee, hot chocolate, or "panela" water and possibly a slice of cheese to dunk in the drink.
